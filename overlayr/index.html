Every surveyor, valuer, and planner has faced the same problem: you have a map — a scanned survey sheet, a PDF layout plan, a development plan extract — and you need to know exactly where it sits on the ground. Overlayr solves this on your phone. A Mobile Alternative to QGIS & ArcGIS Georeferencing

On desktop, georeferencing means the QGIS Georeferencer or the ArcGIS georeferencing tools: load the raster, place ground control points, pick a transformation, export. Powerful — but tied to a workstation and a real learning curve. If what you actually need is to see where a plan sits on the ground and share it, Overlayr runs that same control-point workflow on your Android phone, standing on the site.

Searching for "QGIS mobile" or "ArcGIS mobile"? QGIS itself doesn't run on Android — its companion apps (like QField) focus on field data collection, and Esri's ArcGIS Field Maps does the same on the Esri side. Neither georeferences an image on the phone. That gap — mobile georeferencing of scanned maps, site plans, and PDFs — is exactly what Overlayr is built for.

To be clear: Overlayr is not a full GIS and doesn't try to be. It's the georeferencing-and-overlay step made mobile — and because it exports standard KMZ, ArcGIS-compatible world files, and point ZIP packages, the result flows straight back into Google Earth, QGIS, or ArcGIS when you do need desktop analysis.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a map overlay?

A map overlay is an image — a site plan, drawing, scanned sheet, or another map — placed on top of a base map so you see both together. In GIS, creating an overlay means georeferencing the image: aligning it to real-world coordinates so it sits at its true position and scale. Overlayr does exactly this on Android, overlaying your image on Google satellite imagery with adjustable transparency.

How do I overlay an image on Google Maps on Android?

Install Overlayr from Google Play, import your image (JPG, PNG, or PDF), and place at least 3 control points — matching a point on your image to the same spot on the satellite map. Overlayr then warps the image so it sits correctly on Google satellite imagery. A transparency slider lets you check the alignment against the ground below.

Can I overlay a scanned paper map or an old survey map?

Yes. Scanned paper maps, old survey sheets, layout plans, and PDF drawings are exactly what Overlayr is built for. Scan or photograph the map, import it, and georeference it with control points — the app handles high-resolution files and is memory-optimized for large scans.

How accurate is georeferencing on a phone?

Accuracy depends mainly on how carefully you place control points, not on the device. Overlayr provides magnified precision views while placing each point and live overlay transformation, so with 3 or more well-chosen points on recognizable features you can achieve desktop-grade alignment on satellite imagery.

How do I export to Google Earth (KMZ), ArcGIS, or QGIS?

After georeferencing, choose your export: KMZ opens directly in Google Earth (desktop and Pro) with your image at its true coordinates; an ArcGIS-compatible world file lets ArcGIS and QGIS load the georeferenced image natively; and a point ZIP package carries the image with its georeferencing data for GIS workflows. You can also export high-resolution screenshots for reports.

Does Overlayr work offline?

Yes — Overlayr has offline capability, so you can keep working with your imported images and saved projects in the field. Fresh satellite imagery requires a connection when first loading an area.

Is Overlayr free?

Overlayr is free to download and use from Google Play. Premium (an optional in-app purchase) unlocks unlimited saved projects for professionals who work on many sites.

Which file formats can I import?

JPG, PNG, and PDF. High-resolution images are supported and the app is optimized to stay responsive with large files, so detailed scans of survey sheets and development plans work well.

How does Overlayr compare to Map Over Pro?

They solve different problems. Map Over Pro is an older app built mainly for viewing a custom map overlay while hiking or navigating in remote areas, aligned with two matched points. Overlayr is built for professional georeferencing: 3 or more control points with live warping for an accurate fit, area and distance measurement, and GIS-grade exports — KMZ for Google Earth, an ArcGIS-compatible world file, and a point ZIP package that desktop GIS software reads directly. Overlayr is free to download. And it covers the hiking use case too — georeference a trail or forest map once, then follow your live GPS position on the overlay offline in remote areas.

Can I run QGIS or ArcGIS on my Android phone?

Not the desktop applications themselves. QGIS has mobile companions like QField and ArcGIS has Field Maps, but those are built for field data collection — neither lets you georeference an image on the phone. For mobile georeferencing — fitting a scanned map, site plan, or PDF onto satellite imagery — Overlayr covers that workflow on Android and exports KMZ that both QGIS and ArcGIS can import.

Is there a QGIS or ArcGIS alternative for Android?

For the georeferencing-and-overlay workflow, yes — Overlayr is a free mobile alternative to the QGIS Georeferencer and the ArcGIS georeferencing tools. You place control points on Google satellite imagery just like desktop GCPs and get a fitted overlay in minutes on your phone. For full spatial analysis (buffers, joins, styling, projections), a desktop GIS is still the right tool.

Can Overlayr replace QGIS or ArcGIS?

It replaces the desktop trip for field georeferencing — not the whole GIS. Use Overlayr on-site to georeference plans and check them against the ground, then export KMZ; the file imports directly into Google Earth, QGIS, or ArcGIS whenever you need deeper analysis. Many users pair Overlayr with desktop GIS exactly this way.

Overlayr vs Location Plan Maker — which app do I need?

Use Overlayr when you already have a map or plan (a scanned sheet, PDF drawing, or image) and want to position it on satellite imagery — georeferencing. Use Location Plan Maker Pro when you want to create a new location plan or site map from scratch, with landmark markers, roads, and a branded PDF export.
