The best trail information is often on paper — a forest department map, a trek organizer's route PDF, an old guidebook page. Overlay it on satellite imagery once, and your live GPS position appears right on that map, offline, deep in the hills.

See how it works

Every hiker has been there: the route that actually matters isn't in any app. It's on the laminated map at the forest checkpoint, in the PDF the trek organizer emailed, or in a guidebook printed before smartphones existed. Trail-database apps only know the trails in their catalogue — and in remote areas, that catalogue is thin.

Overlayr puts your live GPS position on the map you actually trust. Photograph or import the trail map, match 3 or more recognizable points — a road junction, a river bend, a peak — to the same spots on satellite imagery, and Overlayr warps the map to fit reality. From that moment, the blue dot moves across your map's trails.

Do the georeferencing at home on Wi-Fi, view the area once so the imagery is loaded, and the whole thing works offline on the trail — GPS needs no mobile network. Adjust transparency to compare the drawn trail against the actual terrain, measure how far the next waypoint is, and drop markers at camps or water points.

How to Use a Paper Trail Map with GPS

1

Capture the map

Photograph the trailhead signboard, scan the guidebook page, or import the trek PDF — JPG, PNG, and PDF all work, including large high-resolution scans.

2

Georeference at home

On Wi-Fi, match 3+ points between the map and satellite imagery — junctions, bridges, peaks. Magnified precision views help you tap exactly.

3

Load the area once

Pan over your route so the imagery is loaded while you still have a connection. After that, the overlay works fully offline.

4

Hike with the blue dot

On the trail, your GPS position tracks across the overlaid map — no signal needed. Catch a wrong turn in 50 meters instead of 5 kilometers.

Hiking Map Overlay — FAQ

Can I use a paper trail map with GPS on my phone?

Yes. Photograph or scan the paper map, import it into Overlayr (JPG, PNG, or PDF), and georeference it by matching 3 or more points on the map to the same spots on satellite imagery — a road junction, a river bend, a peak. From then on, your live GPS position appears directly on the paper map's trails.

Does the hiking overlay work offline, with no mobile signal?

Yes — that's the point. Georeference the map and view the area once while you have a connection (at home on Wi-Fi is easiest). After that, your overlay and the loaded imagery work offline, and GPS itself needs no mobile network — so the blue dot keeps moving on your trail map deep in the hills.

How accurate is the overlay on a trail?

It depends on the quality of the source map and your control points. Choose 3 or more well-spread points on features you can identify precisely on satellite imagery, and the warped overlay will track your GPS position closely enough to keep you on the right trail and catch a wrong turn early. Always carry a backup for serious treks.

What kind of maps can I overlay for hiking?

Anything you can get as an image or PDF: forest department maps, trek organizer route PDFs, old guidebook maps, scanned toposheets, hand-drawn trail sketches, or a photo of the signboard map at the trailhead. Overlayr handles large high-resolution scans.

Is this a Map Over Pro alternative for hiking?

Yes. Map Over Pro is an older app built for the same idea — a custom map overlay for hiking in remote areas, aligned with two matched points. Overlayr is a modern, free alternative: 3 or more control points with live warping for a better fit, distance measurement to the next waypoint, custom markers, and export options if you also want the map in Google Earth.

How is this different from AllTrails-style hiking apps?

Trail-database apps show you their maps of trails in their catalogue. Overlayr shows you your map — the forest department sheet, the trek organizer's PDF, the guidebook page — aligned on real satellite imagery. For lesser-known routes that no app database covers, your paper map plus GPS is often the only reliable option.

Is the hiking map overlay free?

Yes — Overlayr is free to download and use from Google Play. An optional Premium in-app purchase unlocks unlimited saved projects, useful if you keep a library of trek maps.
