Location Plan for Building Permission (India)

Every building-permission file in India needs a location plan — a drawing that shows where the plot sits relative to prominent landmarks, with enough written distances that the Competent Authority can locate the site for inspection. Unlike the site plan (which must be to scale, typically 1:500 or 1:1000), the location plan's job is simple identification: which road, which landmarks, how far.

Location Plan Maker produces exactly this in minutes. Mark the plot, drop markers on the nearby school, temple, and bus stop, label the approach road, add measured distances from a known point, and export a clean PDF for the application file. Because the app draws on real Google Maps imagery, proportions are automatically true to the ground — better than the hand-drawn sketches most files still contain. (Always confirm your local authority's format checklist — e.g. north arrow or specific labels — before submitting.)

Route Map & Location Map for Property Valuation Reports

Indian bank and government-approved valuer report formats require a location-cum-route map of the property — a map that establishes where the property is and how to reach it from a recognizable landmark. For most valuers this still means a Google Maps screenshot with arrows drawn on top: no measurements, no branding, and it looks like exactly what it is.

With Location Plan Maker you mark the property, trace the route from the nearest highway or railway station with measured distances, label the landmarks along the way, and export a professional PDF or PNG with your firm's logo — ready to drop into the report annexure. Saved projects mean a repeat valuation in the same locality takes two minutes, not twenty. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a location plan?

A location plan is a map that shows where a site or plot sits relative to its surroundings — nearby roads, landmarks, and access routes. In Indian building-approval documentation it is a drawing, often not to scale but with written distances, showing prominent landmarks so the authority can locate the site for inspection. It is also a standard annexure in property valuation reports and real-estate brochures.

What is the difference between a location plan, a site plan, and a key plan?

A key plan shows the position of the site within a broader area. A location plan identifies the site using nearby landmarks and written distances, and is usually not required to be to scale. A site plan is a scaled drawing (commonly 1:500 or 1:1000) showing the site boundaries, existing structures, access roads, and natural features. Building-permission applications in India typically require all three.

Does a location plan need to be drawn to scale?

For most Indian building-permission requirements a location plan does not need to be to scale — written distances and recognizable landmarks are what matter. Location Plan Maker draws on real Google Maps imagery, so your plan's proportions are automatically true to the ground and every distance and area label is measured, not estimated.

Can I use it for building-permission documents?

Yes. Mark the plot, drop landmark markers (schools, hospitals, bus stops, temples and more), label the approach roads, add distances, and export a clean PDF — a submission-ready location plan in minutes. Always confirm your local authority's specific format requirements (north arrow, plot dimensions, etc.) before submitting.

Can I make the route map or location map for a property valuation report?

Yes — this is one of the app's most common uses. Indian bank and valuer report formats require a location-cum-route map of the property. With Location Plan Maker you can mark the property, show the route from a known landmark with measured distances, add your firm's logo, and export a professional PDF or PNG for the report annexure.

Does Location Plan Maker work offline?

Yes — after the map of your area has loaded once, you can continue creating and editing plans without an internet connection. GPS positioning also works offline.
